Long ago, the world was known as Elarion, a vast land where kingdoms flourished beneath endless skies and ancient magic flowed through every forest, river, and mountain. During this age, places such as Green Meadow, Peaceful Forest, and Calm Lake truly lived up to their names. Travelers crossed the roads safely, merchants wandered freely between villages, and the people believed the world would remain peaceful forever.

Settlements like Starter Village, Fishing Village, and Lakeside Inn became gathering places for adventurers, hunters, and traders. Farmers sold crops in Farmer's Market, caravans rested at Trading Post, and rangers protected the wilderness from Woodland Outpost. Even the deepest forests and tallest mountains were once filled with life instead of fear.

At the center of the world stood World Tree Peak, a colossal tree believed to connect the heavens, the earth, and every living soul. Its roots stretched beneath the entire continent, carrying magical energy through the land itself. The people worshipped it as the heart of creation, and for centuries, its presence kept the balance between all things.

But over time, the rulers of Elarion became obsessed with power beyond mortal understanding.

Scholars from Mystic Temple studied forbidden magic hidden within ancient texts. The skywatchers of Celestial Bridge searched the stars for knowledge beyond the world. Deep beneath the surface, the arcane researchers who would later vanish into Underground City discovered signs of realms existing beyond reality itself.

They called it *The Beyond*.

It was said to be a place outside the known world — a realm of endless magic, infinite knowledge, and unimaginable power. Many believed that whoever controlled it would become greater than kings, greater than gods.

So the ancient civilizations attempted the impossible.

Massive rituals were performed across the world. Experiments with living crystal began in regions now known as Crystal Cavern and Crystal Forest. Spirit summoning rituals were carried out within Forgotten Shrine and hidden chambers beneath Spirit Realm. Astronomers climbed the peaks of Thunder Peaks searching for a way to pierce the sky itself.

Then came the night that ended the old world forever.

The oceans stopped moving. The winds vanished. Every flame across the continent suddenly went dark. Animals screamed and fled through Pine Forest and Oak Woodland. The waters of Rushing River and Mystic Waterfall stood completely still.

And then the sky broke open.

A massive fracture appeared above World Tree Peak, stretching across the heavens like a wound carved into reality itself. Witnesses described black light pouring from the crack while shapes moved behind it — things no human mind could truly understand.

That moment became known as *The Night the Sky Opened*.

Something entered the world.

No one knows what it was. Entire kingdoms vanished before they could even react. Some cities were abandoned overnight, while others collapsed into madness within days. The balance of the world shattered instantly.

The beautiful northern woodland once known as the Silver Forest transformed into Shadow Woods after the shadows beneath its trees began moving on their own. Travelers claimed they could hear familiar voices whispering from deep within the darkness, calling them further into the forest until they disappeared forever.

A peaceful settlement in the eastern valleys became Haunted Hollow after every resident vanished during a single night. The homes remained untouched. Meals still sat warm on tables. Yet not a single body was ever found. Even now, ghostly voices echo through the hollow after sunset.

Deep underground, strange crystal growths erupted through the mines beneath the world. Entire tunnels became consumed by glowing formations, eventually forming the endless maze now known as Crystal Labyrinth. Miners exposed to the crystals for too long slowly lost their memories, their bodies twisting into unnatural creatures.

The deserts erupted into flame. Lava poured from beneath Scorched Dunes and Volcanic Ridge, creating the infernal depths of Magma Chamber, where the first demons emerged into the mortal world.

Far to the north, unnatural storms buried Frozen Peaks beneath eternal ice. Those who wandered too close to Frozen Abyss Portal were often found frozen solid, their faces locked in expressions of absolute terror.

The world began to fracture not only physically, but spiritually.

The dead refused to remain dead.

Spirits flooded into reality through places that would later become Cursed Graveyard and Spirit Realm. Meanwhile, creatures from beyond reality emerged from the growing darkness surrounding Void Gate, believed to be the first point where the sky itself had torn apart.

Entire floating kingdoms fell from the heavens, leaving behind only the broken remains of Celestial Bridge suspended above the clouds. Ancient fortresses became corrupted strongholds such as Demon Fortress. Even sacred places like Forgotten Shrine and Jade Temple became places of fear and uneasy silence.

Many survivors abandoned the ruins of civilization and attempted to rebuild smaller lives among the remnants of the old world. Camps such as Riverside Camp and Miner's Rest became safe havens for wanderers trying to survive in a world that no longer made sense.

But the scars of the catastrophe never faded.

The fog surrounding Misty Grove never lifted again. Strange whispers continue to echo through Echo Caves. The shimmering lights of Prism Valley are known to drive travelers mad. The waters of Moonlit Shore no longer move naturally beneath the moonlight. And in the deepest waters of Abyssal Trench, ancient sea creatures older than recorded history are said to still sleep beneath the darkness.

Now, generations later, most people know the catastrophe only through legends, rumors, and fragmented stories. Many believe the old world is gone forever.

But adventurers who travel too far eventually realize the terrifying truth.

The world was never healed.

The fracture in the sky still exists.

And somewhere beyond places like Forgotten Crypt Entrance, Astral Plane, and Void Gate…

something is still watching.

Waiting for the sky to open once again.
